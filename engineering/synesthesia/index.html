synesthesia

This project was a collaboration with a student majoring in New Media at SUNY Purchase. Their final project in an experimental sound design class involved a musical performance played over a recording of a poem. To add a visual element to the experience, I designed an Arduino-based device which displayed different colors of light on an LED strip based on the range of notes played on the keyboard used for the performance.

The most significant issue that I ran into during construction was that both reading the MIDI data from the keyboard and writing values to the LED were processor intensive tasks, and could not be handled by a single microcontroller. Instead I divided the tasks among two boards, with one reading an optoisolated MIDI signal through a breakout board and sending corresponding analog signals to a second board, which lit specific ranges of the LED strip according to the data from the first board. A third board was added to control a strobe light with a foot pedal, but was not implemented in the performance below. All of the control electronics were housed in a shoebox, as can be seen above.
